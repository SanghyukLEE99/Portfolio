About Me

"Embrace Innovation, Engineer the Future"

Greetings. I am Sanghyuk Lee, a Data Scientist by trade, armed with a comprehensive background in quantitative economics. Over the years, I have nurtured an expertise in various programming languages including Python, R, and JavaScript. My key interests include the exploration and application of emerging technologies, particularly AI and blockchain, as part of the broader data science landscape.

My academic journey in the field of economics at Washington State University was complemented by a strong curiosity towards computer science. This combination led to a unique intersection where I was able to apply my computer skills to economic problems. This was demonstrated through my projects, notably, my work in predicting inflation rates using ARIMA as the base model for implementing SARIMAX and creating a hybrid predictive model for stock prices utilizing both Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM). These experiences have allowed me to develop a robust understanding of data pipelines, API integration, and the importance of seamless collaboration between front-end and back-end interfaces.

In addition to my academic and project experiences, I have also developed valuable problem-solving skills and resilience during my military service in the Republic of Korea, where I worked on a critical communication program, the Combat Spider Net.

Today, as a Data Scientist, I am eager to apply these skills and experiences to help organizations enhance their performance through strategic data analysis and insight generation. I am deeply familiar with the ever-evolving nature of the IT industry, and I am prepared and excited to navigate through these changes. I invite you to peruse my portfolio to gain a deeper understanding of my past projects and achievements. Please feel free to contact me if you believe there is potential for us to create meaningful work together.
